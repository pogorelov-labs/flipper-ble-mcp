# Hardware Theory — GPIO, Buses & Internal Radios

> **TL;DR —** The STM32WB55 GPIO header (pinout, SPI/I²C/UART/1-Wire/SWD buses, ADC/PWM, 3.3 V logic and power/current limits) plus the internal radio front-ends (CC1101, NFC, LF RFID, IR, iButton, BLE). Explains why heavy work (Wi-Fi, video, GPS) is offloaded to a second MCU on backpacks.
> Boards: [gpio-addons-current.md](gpio-addons-current.md) · DIY: [gpio-addons-potential.md](gpio-addons-potential.md) · pick one: [buying-guide.md](buying-guide.md) · plain-words explainer: [addons-explained.md](addons-explained.md). Part of the [KB](../README.md) · [architecture map](../01-architecture.md).

Everything here is driven by the **STM32WB55RG** SoC: a Cortex-**M4 @ 64 MHz** application core + Cortex-**M0+ @ 32 MHz** radio (BLE) core, sharing **1 MB flash / 256 KB RAM**. The GPIO header and all internal front-ends hang off this one chip — which is exactly why heavy work gets offloaded to a second MCU on backpacks (see bottom).

## GPIO header (18 pins)

All I/O is **3.3 V logic**, routed through **51 Ω series resistors** (ESD protection) and **3.3 V-tolerant only — NOT 5 V-tolerant**. Driving a pin above ~3.6 V can damage the MCU; level-shift 5 V sensors. There are **18 physical pins** but only **~13 are user I/O** (the rest are power/GND).

| Pin | Label | Bus / primary function | ADC | PWM/timer |
|----:|-------|------------------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | +5V | 5 V power out (off by default) | — | — |
| 2 | PA7 | SPI **MOSI** / GPIO | **ADC1_IN12** | TIM1_CH1N, TIM17_CH1 |
| 3 | PA6 | SPI **MISO** / GPIO | **ADC1_IN11** | TIM16_CH1 |
| 4 | PA4 | SPI **CS** / GPIO / DAC_OUT1 | — | LPTIM2_OUT |
| 5 | PB3 | SPI **SCK** / GPIO | — | TIM2_CH2 |
| 6 | PB2 | GPIO (common NRF24 **CE**) | — | LPTIM1_OUT |
| 7 | PC3 | GPIO | **ADC1_IN4** | LPTIM1 (alt) |
| 8 | GND | Ground | — | — |
| 9 | +3V3 | 3.3 V power out (on by default) | — | — |
| 10 | SWC | **SWD** clock (SWCLK / PA14) | — | — |
| 11 | GND | Ground | — | — |
| 12 | SIO | **SWD** data (SWDIO / PA13) / GPIO | — | — |
| 13 | TX (PB6) | **USART1 TX** (default 115200 8N1) | — | TIM16_CH1N |
| 14 | RX (PB7) | **USART1 RX** | — | TIM17_CH1N |
| 15 | PC1 | **I²C SDA** / GPIO / alt **LPUART1 TX** | **ADC1_IN2** | — |
| 16 | PC0 | **I²C SCL** / GPIO / alt **LPUART1 RX** | **ADC1_IN1** | — |
| 17 | PA13/iBtn | **1-Wire** / iButton data | — | — |
| 18 | GND | Ground | — | — |

> Note: docs label TX as PA15 in older diagrams, but the USART1 lines exposed on 13/14 are **PB6/PB7** on current hardware *(verify against your CFW pin map)*. The SWD/1-Wire pin labels (PA13/PA14) and the iButton line are easy to confuse — confirm before wiring.

### Buses — defaults + alternates

| Bus | Default pins | Alternate / notes |
|---|---|---|
| **UART/USART** | USART1 on **13 (TX) / 14 (RX)**, 115200 8N1 | **LPUART1** can be mapped to **15/16** when 13/14 are busy (or for a 2nd serial port) |
| **SPI** (SPI1) | MOSI **2**, MISO **3**, CS **4**, SCK **5** | Pin **6** commonly used as an extra control/CE line (NRF24). Bus is **shared** — see multiboard caveat |
| **I²C** (I2C1) | SDA **15**, SCL **16** | Same pins also serve external sensors; internal I²C bus also hosts onboard peripherals |
| **1-Wire** | **17** | Dallas/iButton + 1-Wire sensors (DS18B20 etc.) |
| **SWD** | SWCLK **10**, SWDIO **12** | Debug/flash the STM32 itself, or drive external ARM targets (Black Magic via DevBoard) |

### ADC — capable pins & considerations
**ADC-capable: pins 2 (PA7), 3 (PA6), 7 (PC3), 15 (PC1), 16 (PC0).** Pins 4/5/6 are **not** ADC inputs. The STM32WB has a **12-bit SAR ADC** (0–4095 over **0–3.3 V**, ~0.8 mV/LSB; configurable 8/10/12-bit). Practical notes:
- Inputs must stay **0–3.3 V** — no negative, no >3.3 V. Use a divider/op-amp front end for larger signals.
- App-level sample rate is firmware/app-limited (the GPIO/sensor apps poll, not stream MS/s); treat it as **slow analog reads**, not a scope/logic-analyzer DAQ.
- **PA4 (pin 4) is also DAC_OUT1** — the one true analog *output* pin (handy for generating reference voltages/waveforms).

### PWM — capable pins
PWM is generated by routing a pin to a timer channel. Usable PWM/timer outputs: **PA7, PA6, PB3, PB2, PC3 (LPTIM), PB6/PB7**. The stock **GPIO app exposes a simple PWM generator** (frequency + duty) typically on a PA7/PA4-class pin; for arbitrary multi-channel PWM you script via FW APIs. Use for servo/LED/motor-driver test signals at 3.3 V levels.

### Power & current budgeting (in practice)
- **3V3 (pin 9):** on by default, **1.2 A max** rail.
- **5V (pin 1):** **1.2 A max**, **OFF by default** → enable at **Main Menu → GPIO → 5V on GPIO → ON** (also auto-on while USB is connected on recent FW). Sourced from **battery boost or USB**; for hungry boards (ESP32 Wi-Fi TX bursts) **power the Flipper from USB** so the draw isn't fighting the LiPo.
- **Per-pin I/O ~20 mA**; **total I/O power ≤ 5 W**. Budget accordingly: an ESP32 doing Wi-Fi can spike to several hundred mA — fine on the 5V rail over USB, marginal on battery, and never through a single signal pin.
- The 5V toggle is a **soft switch** in firmware; if a board "doesn't power on," that toggle (or a USB-only power need) is the usual culprit.

## Internal-radio hardware (the "front ends")

| Subsystem | Hardware | How it connects internally | Notes for theory/RE |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sub-GHz** | TI **CC1101** transceiver, **315/433/868/915 MHz**, **+20 dBm** max | On a **dedicated internal SPI** to the STM32 (separate from the user GPIO SPI); fixed 433-tuned PCB antenna | Configurable ASK/OOK & 2-FSK, data rate, RX bandwidth, deviation. TX bands **region-gated in FW**. External CC1101 over the **GPIO SPI** adds range/SMA → [sub-ghz](../capabilities/sub-ghz.md) |
| **NFC (HF)** | ST **ST25R3916** front-end, **13.56 MHz** | Internal bus to MCU; drives the HF coil antenna | Multi-protocol reader/emulator (ISO14443A/B, MIFARE, FeliCa, NFC-Forum). Some attacks FW-gated → [nfc-rfid](../capabilities/nfc-rfid.md) |
| **RFID (LF)** | **125 kHz analog path** — *no dedicated reader IC* | MCU **drives/reads the carrier directly** (comparator + timer bit-bang) into the LF coil | AM/OOK demod & encode done in **software** → why new LF protocols (EM4100, HID, Indala, T5577 write) ship via FW updates |
| **Infrared** | IR **TX LEDs (940 nm, ~300 mW)** + **RX demod (38 kHz, 950 nm)** | GPIO/timer-driven TX; demodulator RX into MCU | "Learn" captures carrier + timing; universal remotes brute common code DBs → [infrared](../capabilities/infrared.md) |
| **iButton** | **1-Wire on pin 17** | Direct MCU GPIO (open-drain 1-Wire) | Dallas/Cyfral/Metakom read+emulate → [ibutton](../capabilities/ibutton.md) |
| **BLE** | **STM32WB Cortex-M0+ radio core**, BLE **5.4**, +4 dBm | *Same SoC* — M0+ runs the stack, M4 runs apps, talking over an internal mailbox/IPCC | Drives BadBLE/BLE spam/HID. **Coexistence:** BLE and app share one chip + flash/RAM, so the radio core is firmware-managed and can't run an unrelated Wi-Fi stack — hence external ESP32 for Wi-Fi → [security-pentest](../topics/security-pentest.md) |

**Why there's no Wi-Fi:** the STM32WB has **BLE only** (no Wi-Fi PHY, no 5 GHz, no real CPU headroom for it). Anything Wi-Fi/2.4-or-5 GHz, camera, or video must be an **external** module.

## Why backpacks pair the Flipper with a second MCU
The STM32WB55 is modest (M4 @ 64 MHz, 256 KB RAM, BLE-only). Heavy compute — **Wi-Fi (2.4/5 GHz), camera/video, GPS parsing, DVI output** — is **offloaded** to a companion processor (**ESP32** family, **RP2040**) over **UART or SPI**, with the Flipper acting as **UI/host/controller**. That's exactly why the popular add-on boards carry their own SoC, and why you pick a board by *which co-processor + radios you need* — see [gpio-addons-current.md](gpio-addons-current.md) and the [buying-guide.md](buying-guide.md). Tinkering/build details: [tinkering](../topics/tinkering.md).
